Indian National Flag Facts

The Indian National Flag, designed in 1921 by Pingali Venkayya, symbolizes courage (saffron), peace and truth (white), and prosperity (green), with the navy-blue Ashoka Chakra representing the wheel of law and progress.

About India’s Independence Day

Every year on 15 August, India celebrates the end of nearly 200 years of British colonial rule, achieved in 1947 after decades of non-violent resistance, mass movements, and countless sacrifices. The Indian Independence Act, passed by the British Parliament, came into effect at the midnight of 14–15 August, marking the birth of two sovereign nations — India and Pakistan — as independent dominions within the British Commonwealth.

On that historic morning, India’s first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, hoisted the tricolour atop the Red Fort in Delhi, delivering his legendary “Tryst with Destiny” speech — a moment etched forever in the nation’s memory. This tradition continues each year, with the Prime Minister addressing the nation after the ceremonial flag-raising, broadcast live across the country.

Independence Day is more than a commemoration — it is a tribute to the vision, courage, and unity of the freedom movement. Schools, institutions, and communities across India celebrate with flag-hoisting ceremonies, cultural programs, patriotic songs, and parades, while the tricolour waves proudly in every corner of the country, symbolising the values of democracy, justice, and equality that continue to guide the Republic.
